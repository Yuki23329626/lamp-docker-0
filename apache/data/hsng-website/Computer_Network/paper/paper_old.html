A New On-line Paradigm for Developing Courses, Teaching, and Learning: A Project in the Asynchronous Learning Environment at National Chung Cheng University

Ren-Hung Hwang and http://exodus.cs.ccu.edu.tw/~rhhwang/ M. S. Chao National Chung Cheng University, Chia-Yi, Taiwan
Email: rhhwang@cs.ccu.edu.tw

Keywords

World Wide Web, Asynchronous Learning, Distance Learning

Abstract

The pervasion of technology provides a wide range of the possibilities for learning. In addition to the regular classroom setting, there are many other ways for learners to learn. This project is to create an asynchronous learning environment which provides students and instructors the flexibility in learning and teaching activities. Generally speaking, the purposes of this project are to: 1. design and develop the asynchronous learning environment; 2. investigate the advantages and limitations of this learning system.

Subjects consisted of 42 junior and senior undergraduate students. One instructor and three teaching assistants are responsible for this project, including creating necessary tools for this learning environment, a three-hour classroom teaching and some off-line assistance.

Results indicated that, first of all, this learning system could be used to catch up learning for those who fell behind in the regular classroom learning. Second, some tools created in this project, such as the chat room, bulletin board, e-mail, etc. did facilitate the interaction between students and instructors. Third, teachers and students got feedback much sooner compared with the traditional classroom teaching.

1. Introduction

Educators have always been attracted to issues that involved with students' learning achievement and treatment. Since technology rapidly advances in teaching and learning activities, the problems of individual differences can be taken care of more effectively. Owing to the computer network technology, the individualized instruction becomes more pervasive. However, because of some environmental and cost considerations, some of the teaching has to be carried out in the mass instruction format. In this case, designing the flexible learning environment is very desirable because for one reason, it can allow students to catch up some learning which they have missed in the classroom, and on the other hand, students' achievement will be enhanced via the utilization of computer-based technologies (CBL) (Oliver, R.G. 1997).

Research has reported that the asynchronous learning environment has great impact on students' learning. In this learning environment, it was investigated that the peer interaction was increased compared with in the traditional learning environment (Ahern, 1992). Factors that affect the creation of the asynchronous learning environment were examined in several research studies (Sener, 1996; Terrell, 1996; Sugar, et al. 1995; Newbold, 1993). It has been recognized that to create the asynchronous learning environment must consider the following elements: the access to large information, developing effective teaching and learning activities to suit individual needs, transmitting contents via multiplicity forms of media, the high levels of learner control, etc. Several studies indicated that lower costs and increasing capabilities of hardware and communications technology would lead directly to the development and use of audio, graphics, and full-motion video programs for distant learners in this learning environment.

This paper will describe the current project at National Chung Cheng University in Taiwan into the design and development of the asynchronous learning environment. This project involves the use of computer-based learning in the design and development process of interactive multimedia as a means to bring about enhanced learning outcomes among 42 junior and senior undergraduate students in the course of " Computer Networks ". Learning contents were delivered via the forms of text, graphics, animation, and real audio. Aside from the three-hour classroom learning, students enrolled in this course can get access to the learning materials at their conveniences. The following section will explain the details of this project.

2. Development of the Asynchronous Learning Environment

Generally speaking, the asynchronous learning environment provides students the teaching materials and some tools for registration, instruction, and discussion. The ultimate goal of this project was to offer interactive resources available at the learner's convenience. Several tools were developed in this project to make the asynchronous learning more effective and user-friendly. They are schematically described in Figure 1.

Figure 1: Tools provided in the project.

Tools for the asynchronous learning environment

It is well recognized that computer-based technologies provides not only the means for effective instructional strategies, but also intelligent and convenient management. In the project that we are working now, both instructional and managerial tools were developed to support teaching and learning. In the World Wide Web (WWW) homepage of this project, different icons with hyperlinks are shown to direct readers to tools that they are interested.

Registration Tool

This tool is designed for the ease of class management. Students are required to add or withdraw this course and provide personal information, such as student id, email address, and personal homepage address, via this tool. On the other hand, instructors could make on-line inquiries about the most updated name list of this class, send email to a student by simply clicking the name of the student, or link to the student's homepage via hyperlink.

Announcement Tool

Instructors could post news or announcements via this tool. Things like the names of the absent students, students' involvement in the asynchronous learning environment, changes of classroom hours, etc. would all be posted here and students could get the posted information at any time.

Lecture Notes

Instead of putting the slides of lecture notes on the Web, all the lecture contents and supplemental materials are carefully designed by the use of multiplicity forms of media, such as text, graphics, animation, and real-time audio. Students are able to get more detailed information that are not covered in the class due to the limited lecture time. The use of animation helps the student understand some network protocols by viewing how protocols operate in real networks. Foe example, the operation of CSMA/CD protocol, which is the medium access control protocol of Ethernets, is animated to show why collisions happen, how long to detect a collision, how to select a random time to wait according to the exponential backoff algorithm. Some students, such as foreign students, may have difficult time on understanding the materials delivered in class due to language problem. Therefore, the voice of the instructor was recorded during each lecture time and stored in the real audio server. The students can listen to the audio via real-time audio player as they browse the lecture contents. The real-time audio tools we used are developed by Progressive Networks.

Discussion Tool

We believe that the interaction and communication between students and instructors and among students themselves play an important role in the learning process. Therefore, several discussion tools are provided for students to be able to interact with instructors as well as among themselves.

  • BBS

BBS is one of the most popular network tools in Universities in Taiwan. Many students spend several hours on BBS to read posted news and talk to friends. We have observed that most Taiwanese students rather asking questions on BBS than in the class. Therefore, we provide a network-course board on our BBS in which students can post anything related to this course. Instructors will monitor the board and answer questions that posed by students. In fact, we find that BBS provides a very effective environment for students to be able to interact and communicate among themselves because some students will try to provide their answers (thoughts) before the instructors get the time to answer the questions. Some of the thoughts are so creative and fruitful that even the instructors cannot think of. This phenomena may due to the two factors: first, the students feel more comfortable to express their opinions on BBS than in class or face-to-face discussion; second, the traditional Chinese culture makes the students quite nervous in class.

  • News

One of the disadvantages of BBS is lack of multimedia support. Therefore, it limits the way that students express their thoughts. Since the news tool of Netscape supports MIME format, we have also provided a local news group for students to post their questions.

  • Chat room

Many researchers have reported that tutoring also plays an important role in learning process. Therefore, we have developed a WWW-based chat room (using JAVA) which can be used for interactions and communications among instructors and more than one students. Similar to office hours, the instructors will announce when they will enter the chat room. Students who have questions can then enter the chat room during that time. Unlike BBS and News, chat room provides synchronous tutoring which seems to be a more effective learning tool for some students. Since some students have difficult time on typing Chinese characters, we are planning to add an audio-based chat room, such as Internet Phone and Netsacpe's Cool Talk.

  • FAQ

Some questions posted in BBS, News, and chat room are so frequently asked that we feel a FAQ tool is necessary to alleviate repeated answers. Instructors can summarize some frequently asked questions and their answers and then post on the WWW via this tool.

  • E-Mail

The E-Mail tool is provided to facilitate students and instructors to send mails. Students or instructors may want to send mail to a group of people, e.g., students want to send mail to instructors (including tutors) to ask questions, or instructors may want to send mail to teaching assistants or all students. By the use of CGI and form, the E-Mail tool makes sending mail to a predefined group much easier.

Homework Assignments

All the homework and programming assignments are posted here. The written homework is posted first. A FAQ is then posted which contains answers to all of the questions related to the homework posted in BBS, News, and chat room. After the homework is due, the suggested answers are then posted. For the programming assignments, similar process is conducted except that some source codes for demonstration are also posted. Students can turn in their written homework in class or use the turnin software available in UNIX. However, students are required to turn in their programs using the turnin software.

3. Evaluation

Students' achievement will be evaluated by paper-and-pencil tests and the on-line quiz. Besides, the project itself will be evaluated via formative and summative evaluation. Formative evaluation is an on-going process and most of the students' opinions will be taken as meaningful feedforward to revise the project from time to time, such that the project can suit the needs of individual students. A questionnaire survey will be administered at the end of the semester to collect some data to analyze the advantages and limitations of this project.

4. Recommendation for Further Studies

Instructional and computer-based technologies contribute a lot in creating student-centered learning environments, and as university teaching staff, we are oblighed to push toward more open and varied instructional delivery modes (Oliver, 1997). Further studies are suggested to group students based on the gender, entry behavior, grades n prerequisites, etc. to compare their learning achievement as well as learning attitude as the basic data to understand the advantages of different learning environments for different levels of students.
