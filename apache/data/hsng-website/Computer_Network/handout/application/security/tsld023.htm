Public-key Algorithm

  • First proposed by W.Diffie and M.E. Hellman

  • Asymmetric keys: Encryption (public) key is different with Decryption (private) key

  • The Encryption algorithm E and the decryption algorithm D should meet the following requirements.

    • 1. D(E(P)) = P
    • 2. It is exceedingly difficult to deduce D from E
    • 3. E cannot be broken by a chosen plaintext attack.
