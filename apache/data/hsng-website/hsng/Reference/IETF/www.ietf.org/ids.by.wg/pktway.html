"The End-to-End (EEP) PacketWay Protocol for High-Performance Interconnection of Computer Clusters", Danny Cohen, C. Lund, T. Skjellum, T. McMahon, R. George, 10/14/1997. (55164 bytes)
PktWay is an open family of specifications for inter-networking high performance System Area Networks (SANs) and high performance Local Area Networks (LANs) into computing clusters.
"Part-1 of The Router-to-Router (RRP) PacketWay Protocol for High-Performance Interconnection of Computer Clusters", Danny Cohen, C. Lund, T. Skjellum, T. McMahon, R. George, 10/23/1997. (73438 bytes)
The PktWay protocol is introduced in the 'The End-to-End (EEP) PacketWay Protocol for High-Performance Interconnection of Computer Clusters'. This document defines the basic part (Part 1) of the Router-to-Router protocol (RRP) of PacketWay. The shorter 'PktWay' is used for 'PacketWay'.
